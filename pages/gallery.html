BIOGRAPHY
Alexander Iverssen (born 11 September 1991), better known by his stage name dj Eyedrops (is a Norwegian DJ, songwriter, and record producer. He garnered international attention with his December 2013 remix of the track "Down the road" by Ed Sheeran and his December 2014 single “Slowly" featuring Conrad Sewell. In late 2015, he became the fastest artist to reach 1 billion streams on Spotify, and by June 2016, he had reached 2 billion streams on Spotify. Dj Eyedrops has since released several singles, such as "Stole the Show", "Here for You" and "Stay" in 2015, "It Ain't Me" in 2017 and a 2019 remix of "Higher Love" performed by Whitney Houston, all of which have debuted on several international charts. His debut album, Cloud Nine, was released on 13 May 2016. Dj eyedrops became the first house music producer to perform at an Olympics closing ceremony on 21 August 2016 at the 2016 Rio Olympics. In March 2018, Billboard ranked the dj 3rd on its 2018 ranking of dance musicians titled Billboard Dance 100. In 2018, he was ranked 32 on DJ Mag's top 100 DJs of the world, and in October 2019 he was ranked 42. Dj Eyedrops was born on 11 September 1991 in Singapore to Norwegian parents and raised in Tønsberg.
